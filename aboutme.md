### Hello, I'm Lee

I have a broad background in ecology, microbiology, water quality, data science and coding. I am particularly interested in applying my knowledge, skills and interests to tackling real-world issues and preserving natural resources. 
I earned a PhD at the University of Colorado Boulder where I focused on microbial adaptations in harsh polar ecosystems. I later spent time as a postdoctoral researcher leading projects on algal biofuels and drinking water microbiology. I acted as the Microbial Ecologist at the National Ecological Observatory Network (NEON) from 2014-2021 where I managed the soil microbial sampling program, including field sampling and quality assurance for a suite of microbial data products. Later, I was a Product Owner at the USGS and helped develop new open-source software that improved accessibility and use of publicly available water data. Also at the USGS I developed methods to aid in the detection of contaminants such as PFAS in aquatic microorganisms.
