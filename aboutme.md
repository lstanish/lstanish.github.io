### Hello, I'm Lee

I am an ecologist, microbiologist, water quality specialist, and data scientist. I love to apply my knowledge, skills and interests to tackle real-world issues and support thriving ecosystems.

I earned a PhD at the University of Colorado Boulder where I focused on microbial adaptations in polar environments. I developed a love of diatoms and algae that occupy the 'benthos'. Working with the McMurdo Dry Valleys Long-Term Ecological Resaerch Program (LTER)  taught me the importance of data management when planning and executing projects. I continued exploring microbes and ecology as a postdoctoral researcher leading projects on algal biofuels and drinking water microbiology.

My first professional position was as the Microbial Ecologist at the National Ecological Observatory Network (NEON), where I managed the soil microbial monitoring program from sample collection to data dissemination. It is here that I honed my data science skills and developed worklfows for standardizing national-scale data management practices. I particularly enjoyed engaging with the dedicated and hard-working field staff and the research community. 

Later, I was a Product Owner with the USGS Water Mission Area where I helped to modernize aging tech infrastructure and improve public access to critical water data. I also returned to the laboratory and developed methods to understand how emerging contaminants such as PFAS are taken up and transformed by aquatic microbes.
