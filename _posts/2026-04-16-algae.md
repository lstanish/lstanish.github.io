Most of our blue planet is water, where trees and vascular plants can't grow. Algae fill that critical primary production niche in aquatic environments: studies estimate that microscopic algae generate [40](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/BF00014586)-[50](https://www.science.org/doi/abs/10.1126/science.281.5374.237)% of all oxygen worldwide. I became interested in algae while studying abroad with [Sea Semester](www.sea.edu), where I investigated latitudinal changes in abundances of the marine cyanobacteria Trichodesmium sp. My experiences sparked a life-long interest in the amazing microscopic life that drives entire food webs.
